# P2.1 LawDesk Core Architecture

- Date: 2026-07-10
- Author: LawDesk Team
- Scope: High-level product and technical architecture definitions for LawDesk V1 and the planned V2. This document defines core concepts, relationships, UI constraints, and boundaries for follow-up design tasks P2.2 / P2.3 / P2.4.
- Constraints: No code, schema, table, API or UI implementation in this document.

**1. Core Positioning (LawDesk V1 / V2)**

- LawDesk is an AI-native operating system for independent lawyers. Its primary goal is to reduce cognitive load, improve quality of legal work, and let AI participate continuously in the lifecycle of a Matter.
- V1: Deliver a dependable, lightweight OS that centralizes case work around a single source-of-truth (`Matter`), provides rule-based orchestration (`Today`), and practical tooling for everyday lawyer workflows (evidence, research, documents, execution).
- V2: Evolve toward deeper AI-assisted workflows, richer automation (dispatch engine, priority scoring, recommendations), integrations, and multi-tenant/scale concerns while preserving lawyer-in-the-loop principles.

**2. Matter Is The Single Core Object**

- `Matter` (案件) is the aggregate root and the only core business object that all user-facing operations reference.
- All persistent records (materials, evidence, documents, tasks, AI records) belong to exactly one `Matter` and never exist as top-level loose entities.
- Business actions enter the system through `Matter` (Matter Workspace) to preserve traceability and auditability.

**3. Work Item: Concrete Unit Of Work Inside a Matter**

- A `Work Item` is a discrete actionable item within a `Matter`. Examples: "Collect bank statement", "Draft complaint", "Confirm witness contact".
- `Work Item` captures what needs to be done, why, who is responsible, and its current blocking/ready state.
- Work Items are ephemeral or persistent depending on the workflow; they are domain primitives consumed by the scheduling layer (`Today`).

**4. Artifact: Outcome Produced By Work Item**

- An `Artifact` is the tangible output of a completed Work Item: a Document draft, Evidence record, Research note, Fact, Issue, or Argument entry.
- Artifacts are stored and versioned under the `Matter` and referenced by Work Items when relevant.
- Artifacts represent evidence and deliverables; they are readable, reviewable, and approvable by the lawyer.

**5. Today: The Scheduling & Orchestration Layer**

- `Today` is a read-only, rule-driven scheduling layer that aggregates `Work Items` and `Artifacts` across Matters to produce prioritized actions, waiting items, and summary cards for lawyers.
- It is explicitly NOT business state; it reflects business state owned by `Matter` and its children.
- `Today` runs deterministic rules (priority, ETA, stage hints) and surfaces `Next Actions`, `Waiting`, `Completed`, and `Active Matters`.

**6. Workspace: Matter Work View**

- `Workspace` is the UI and UX context for a single `Matter`. It organizes views by role-focused tabs: Overview, Materials, Evidence, Documents, Research, Execution, Timeline, etc.
- Work Items and Artifacts are visible and actionable inside the `Workspace` from the lawyer's perspective.

**7. AI: A Processor, Not A Decision-Maker**

- AI functions as a data processor and assistant: summarization, classification, recommendation, drafting, and organization.
- AI may propose Work Items and generate Artifacts (drafts), but it must not make final legal decisions or take irreversible actions without lawyer approval.
- All AI outputs require lawyer confirmation before persisting as authoritative artifacts or closing Work Items.

**8. Lawyer: Driver And Approver**

- Lawyers are the primary actors: they drive prioritization, review AI proposals, approve artifacts, and make legal decisions.
- The product enforces human-in-the-loop for all critical steps (evidence confirmation, document finalization, case closure, deletion).

**9. Product Language: Hide Technical Terms**

- The UI must not expose internal engineering terms such as `Work Item` or `Artifact` to end users.
- Use domain-friendly terms that lawyers understand (see section 10).

**10. Lawyer-Facing Terminology (kept in the UI)**

- The lawyer interface uses domain labels only. Examples to display to users:
  - 案件 (Matter)
  - 证据整理 (Evidence Workspace)
  - 法律检索 (Legal Research)
  - 起诉状 (Complaint / Pleading)
  - 代理词 (Pleadings / Advocate Brief)
  - 庭审准备 (Hearing Preparation)
  - 执行 (Enforcement / Execution)

**11. Core Object Relationship**

- Relationship diagram (logical):

  Today → Matter → Work Item → Artifact

- Explanation:
  - `Today` observes and aggregates state from many Matters.
  - Each `Matter` contains multiple `Work Items` representing tasks and blockers.
  - Completing a `Work Item` produces one or more `Artifacts`.

**12. V1 Scope: What We Do NOT Do**

- V1 intentionally avoids:
  - Heavy ML-dependent pipelines that require model training and large infra.
  - Cross-matter automated changes without lawyer approval.
  - New persistent tables or schema changes that alter the stable domain model.
  - Fully autonomous agents that take irreversible actions.
  - Complex multi-tenant billing, rights management, and enterprise RBAC (kept simple initially).

**13. V2 Extension Directions**

- V2 may add:
  - A dispatch/decision engine that supports richer rules and learning-driven priorities.
  - Workflow automation templates, retry semantics, and SLA tracking.
  - Better AI-human handoff flows (annotations, RAG augmentation, feedback loop for models).
  - Integrations: document storage, courts, e-sign, and evidence ingestion pipelines.
  - Audit and compliance enhancements (immutability, WORM storage) for production readiness.

**14. Boundaries For Follow-up Design Work (P2.2 / P2.3 / P2.4)**

- P2.2 — `Today` Rules & Dispatch Engine:
  - Define rule DSL, priority scoring, partial vs full aggregation, API contracts for read-only dashboards.
  - Must not change domain schema. Focus on read-only services, configuration-driven rules, and deterministic behavior.

- P2.3 — `Matter` Workspace UX Specification:
  - Define detailed interactions for Materials, Evidence, Documents, Timeline, and Lawyer flows.
  - Define UI labels, guardrails, and confirmation flows for AI outputs.

- P2.4 — `AI Runtime` Integration & Safety:
  - Define AI service contracts, input-output schemas, transform layers (sanitization, trimming), and human-in-the-loop approvals.
  - Define operational concerns: rate limits, caching, logging, and error handling.

---

## Appendix: Principles & Rules

- Keep `Matter` as the single source of truth. Do not surface raw database or table names to users.
- Prefer small, auditable changes; prioritize lawyer control over automation.
- Design for incremental deployment: V1 first, then iterative V2 features behind flags.
